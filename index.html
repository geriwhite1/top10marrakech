Top 10 Things to Do in Marrakech and the Best Time to Visit Each

Marrakech is a truly special city! Just one step into the city, and it feels like what old and new are all fused together. Walking through the crowded bazaar, with all sorts of odd things to see, fragrant spices permeate your senses while the taste of music reflects all around you.

This is the city that is alive and young. You can do a lot of things here in Marrakech! The first thing in your mind will be what's hot and when to visit, If you are planning to visit this city.

Well, the best time to visit Marrakech is generally during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). This guide helps you to explore some of the best experiences of this magnificent city.

01. Immerse Yourself in the Spice Souk (Souk Attarine)

Spick souk is one of the major top 10 marrakech attractions. While the maze of main souks crisscrosses, head in the other direction towards the Souk Attarine, a fragrant spice market, bright with colourful pyramids of saffron, cumin, and ginger, as well as innumerable other exotic spices. You can talk to the vendors, and learn all about different blends. You can also pick some fragrant souvenirs to carry the taste of Marrakech home with you.

Best Time to Visit: Morning or late afternoon to avoid the midday heat and crowds.

02. Discover the Serenity of the Saadian Tombs

Stepping back in time to the Saadian Tombs, a hidden treasure in the Medina, is an engaging activity indeed. Participated in the Saadian dynasty, these tombs, which are elegantly set up, have stupendous Moroccan tilework and detailed carvings. After being sealed for centuries, they are a wonderful testimony to the city's artistic history.

Best Time to Visit: Early in the morning, when all is quiet and serene, and groups have not yet arrived.

03. Find Tranquility at the Anima Garden

The Anima Garden (Andre Heller Garden) is an exciting and magical garden located not far from Marrakech, where one can escape the noise and pollution of the city sunshine. Austrian artist Andr√© Heller designed the garden set in a surreal landscape filled with exotic plants, sculptures, and bright colors. Indeed, a fantastically uplifting experience!

Best Time to Visit: Late afternoon, when the light softens, and the garden glows magically.

04. Unwind in a Traditional Hammam

Partake in the experience of the authentic Moroccan hammam. The public bathhouses present an age-old bathing ritual in reality. You can either visit the local hammam to enjoy the culture or spend a pampering day at a more luxurious spa. You will later feel rejuvenated and fresh.

Best Time to Visit: Any time of the year, but please book in advance. Mostly, this applies to popular spas.

05. Embrace the Charm of a Riad Stay

As you stated, honestly, staying in a riad is part of the Marrakech experience. Homes in Morocco traditionally have known the incredibly beautiful in-house courtyards as well as glorious aristocratic architecture to be the habitat for catching harmonious silence and serenity, away from the energetic life of the great city. Furthermore, it is the personal touch and home-cooked meals that add to the experience.

Best Time to Visit: Throughout the year, riads are delightful, but it is necessary to book beforehand, especially during peak seasons.

06. Savor Authentic Moroccan Tagines

Every tourist has to sample Moroccan food. Look for local restaurants and try out different types of tagine-slow-cooking stews of tender meats and vegetables, aromatic spices. Lamb with prunes, chicken with preserved lemons and olives: each one is storytelling through unique ingredients.

Best Time to Visit: Lunch or dinner: take your time enjoying the slow-cooked dishes.

07. Witness the Sunset Spectacle from a Rooftop Cafe

Rooftop cafes in Marrakech become heaps during the dawn period, at evening, boasting majestic city view saffron tones in sky. Hunt for an ideal secluded area, order not-less-than mint-tea and watch the metamorphosis of caftan colors into a spectacular sunset paint the Medina.

Best Time to Visit: You can visit this place before sunset to catch the golden light blanketing the city.

08. Explore the Grandeur of the Royal Palace of Marrakech (Dar el Makhzen)

You can enjoy watching the grandeur of the royal gates and a mighty view of the Royal Palace. Dar el Makhzen is testimony to the rich history and architectural grandeur of this country.

Best Time to Visit: Day although most probably limited to access because of royal activities.

09. Enjoy Live Gnawa Music

Glisten to the sweet resonances of gnawa music, a form of Moroccan music, African in origin but Western-derived. Search for performances in local cafes or cultural centers, definitely a real and soulful experience. These hypnotic melodies and percussive beats are very mesmerizing sometimes.

Best Time to Visit: Evenings, as many performances take place after dinner.

10. Wander Through the Gardens of the Menara

Escape from the city and discover peace within the ancient olive grove, consisting of a vast reflecting pool offering iconic views of the Atlas Mountains in the distance at the Menara Gardens. But unlike the alienation, here, people flock to spend time. It's a favorite with the locals and tourists alike at dusk.

Best Time to Visit: Beautiful light falls after the sun begins to set late afternoon, or rather at twilight hour.
